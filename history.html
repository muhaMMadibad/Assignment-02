History

  • Ancient Origins

    Karachi's history dates back to ancient times. The area has been inhabited by various civilizations, including the Indus Valley Civilization, which existed around 2500 BCE.

    Arab and Persian Influence

    In the 7th century CE, the region came under Arab and later Persian influence, becoming an important trading and port city along the Indian Ocean trade routes.

    Colonial Era

    In the 19th century, Karachi became a part of British India and a prominent port city due to its strategic location on the Arabian Sea.
    Partition and Independence
    In 1947, Karachi became the capital of Pakistan following the partition of India. It played a crucial role in the early years of the newly formed nation.
    Economic Center
    Karachi has long been the economic center of Pakistan, housing key industries, including manufacturing, finance, and commerce.
